# Pricing and monetization

## Initial model

Skribly is planned as a one-time purchase rather than a required subscription.

Working launch hypothesis:

- free/private tester builds
- early-access lifetime price in India: ₹499–₹999
- international early-access price: US$9.99–US$19.99
- raise pricing after reliability and polish are proven

These are hypotheses, not committed public prices.

## Principles

- local features remain usable without recurring fees
- future cloud sync may be an optional paid service
- do not put core usability behind recurring rent
- payment provider fees and support/refund cost must be modeled before final pricing
- Merchant-of-Record services may simplify international tax handling but are expensive for low-ticket products

## Licence delivery

The first paid system may use a signed licence token validated locally. Avoid a permanent always-online requirement. Device limits, transfers, refunds, and offline recovery require a separate licence-system design before implementation.
