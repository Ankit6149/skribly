# India GST and income-tax working notes

**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-20  
**Owner location assumed for planning:** Delhi, India  
**Important:** This is a planning memo, not legal, GST, accounting, or income-tax advice. A Chartered Accountant must verify the final sales flow before paid launch.

## Business form

An individual may begin selling software without forming a private limited company. Revenue and expenses may be treated as business/professional activity under the individual's PAN, subject to proper records, invoicing, tax returns, banking, and platform requirements.

## GST baseline

CBIC material states a general GST registration threshold of ₹20 lakh aggregate turnover under a PAN, with exceptions under section 24 and other rules. Do not rely on the threshold alone.

Potential complications include:

- inter-State supplies
- supplies through an e-commerce operator required to collect TCS
- which party is merchant/seller of record
- export-of-services conditions
- voluntary registration
- place-of-supply classification
- whether the software transaction is treated as a service/licence in the specific flow

A direct domestic website checkout, Microsoft/Apple store sale, and Merchant-of-Record international sale can have different tax and documentation outcomes.

## GST rate

Software/digital-service transactions are commonly discussed at 18% GST, but the exact classification and whether a displayed consumer price is tax-inclusive must be confirmed for Skribly's licence and delivery structure.

## International sales

Exports of services can be zero-rated when statutory export conditions are met. Registered exporters may use LUT/bond procedures where applicable. Currency realization, customer location, place of supply, and Merchant-of-Record contracts matter.

## Income tax

Income tax is generally based on taxable profit, not a fixed percentage of each sale:

```text
sales and licence revenue
− payment/store fees
− refunds
− signing/developer memberships
− hosting/domain
− eligible software and marketing expenses
− other allowable business expenses
= business profit before applicable tax adjustments
```

The profit is combined with other income and taxed under the applicable individual rules. Keep invoices, payout statements, foreign-remittance records, processor fees, and expense proofs from day one.

## Required pre-launch CA questions

1. Do the planned domestic direct sales require GST registration before the ₹20 lakh threshold because of inter-State or e-commerce rules?
2. For Microsoft Store, Apple, Razorpay, and any Merchant of Record, who is legally the supplier/seller of record?
3. What SAC/classification and GST rate should appear on invoices?
4. Should consumer prices be GST-inclusive?
5. What invoicing records are required for Indian and foreign buyers?
6. Do foreign sales qualify as export of services and what LUT/FIRC/BRC records are needed?
7. Which ITR form and accounting method should be used?
8. Is a separate current account or sole-proprietorship registration advisable?
9. Are TDS/TCS deductions expected from any platform?
10. What refund and credit-note treatment is required?
