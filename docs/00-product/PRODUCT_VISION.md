# Product vision

## One-line promise

**Put a thought directly on anything on your computer. It will be waiting when you return.**

## Problem

Conventional notes are detached from the situations that make them useful. Users must remember to open a notes app, find the right notebook, understand old context, and manually connect a note back to a webpage, application, file, or task. Over time, unrelated notes become mixed together and lose their value.

## Solution

Skribly creates a persistent visual annotation layer over Windows and macOS. A user can type, handwrite, draw, highlight, point, pin, check off, or schedule a reminder directly in the relevant context. Skribly stores the contextual relationship and restores the annotation when that context returns.

## The emotional product

Skribly should feel like peeling a small piece of paper, leaving a thought for future-you, and finding it in exactly the same place later. It is not a dashboard-first productivity system. It is a quiet, tactile memory layer.

## Core audience

- students annotating PDFs, educational websites, and project work
- developers leaving contextual implementation notes
- designers marking interfaces without changing source files
- researchers connecting thoughts to documents and webpages
- professionals managing repetitive workflows across apps
- touchscreen/stylus users who think by handwriting

## Non-goals for v1

- team collaboration
- cloud sync
- AI generation or summarization
- OCR or screen reading
- full whiteboard replacement
- project management
- mobile companion
- Linux support
- app-store dependence

## Success condition

A new user understands the benefit from a 15-second demonstration and keeps Skribly installed because it saves them from repeatedly reconstructing context.
